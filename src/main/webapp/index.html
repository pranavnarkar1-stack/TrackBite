  • Understand Your Individual Calorie Needs
    • Your daily calorie requirement depends on your age, gender, height, weight, activity level, and metabolic rate.
    • General averages: Adult females typically need 1600-2400 calories/day; adult males 2200-3000 calories/day. These vary significantly.
  • Prioritize Nutrient-Dense Foods
    • Fruits and Vegetables: Aim for at least 5 portions a day. They are low in calories, high in fiber, vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants, promoting fullness and overall health.
  • Practice Portion Control
    • Be mindful of serving sizes. Even healthy foods can contribute to excess calories if consumed in large quantities.
    • Measure or weigh portions, especially for calorie-dense foods like nuts, oils, and grains.
  • Do Stay Hydrated with Water:
    • Drink plenty of water throughout the day. Sometimes, thirst can be mistaken for hunger.
    • Drinking water before meals can help you feel fuller.
  • Do Read Food Labels:
    • Pay attention to calorie counts per serving, as well as macronutrient breakdown (carbohydrates, protein, fat), added sugars, and sodium.
  • Don't Consume Excessive Added Sugars:
    • Avoid sodas, sweetened juices, sports drinks, and overly sweet coffee/tea. These are "empty calories" that provide little to no nutritional value and don't promote satiety.
  • Don't Overdo Unhealthy Fats and Fried Foods:
    • Be wary of creamy sauces & gravies, which are often made with butter, cream, or excessive oil, adding hundreds of calories without much nutritional benefit.
  • Don't Rely Heavily on Refined Grains:
    • Limit consumption of white bread, white pasta, and "maida" (refined flour) items. These are low in fiber and nutrients compared to their whole-grain counterparts and can cause rapid blood sugar spikes and don't keep you full for long.
  • Don't Engage in Mindless Eating:
    • Avoid eating while distracted (e.g., watching TV, using your phone, working on the computer).
    • Don't eat out of boredom or stress; find alternative coping mechanisms.
  • Don't Ignore Your Body's Signals:
    • Don't force yourself to finish everything on your plate if you're already full.
    • Conversely, don't ignore true hunger for extended periods, as it can lead to binging later.
